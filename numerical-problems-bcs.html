\chapter{Numerical Problems}

The most emph{Important part }in solving numerical mathematical problems is \emph{Knowing When to use which formula!}. Here We will discuss some important day to day formula.
\section{Numerical Formula}
\begin{itemize}
    \item $a^{2}-b^{2}=(a+b)(a-b)$
    \item $(a+b)^{2}=a^{2}+b^{2}+2ab$
    \item $(a-b)^{2}=a^{2}+b^{2}-2ab$
    \item $4ab=(a+b)^{2}(a-b)^{2}$
    \item $(a+b)^{3}=a^{3}+b^{3}+3a^{2}b+3ab^{2}$
    \item $(a+b)^{3}=a^{3}-b^{3}-3a^{2}b+3ab^{2}$
    \item $(a+b+c)^{2}=a^{2}+b^{2}+c^{2}+2(ab+bc+ca)$
\end{itemize}
This are the most used formula we will find in different problems. Make sure to memorize these formula. Now it's time to solve some problems.
\section{Digit Related Problems}
\begin{itemize}
\item\textbf{1. A, B, C, D, E are 5 consecutive numbers in increasing order, Deleting one from the set decreased the sum of the remaining numbers by 20\% of the sum of 5. Which one of the number is deleted from the set? }\emph{[BB(officer)-2015]} 
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Solution:}
let the first number be $x$.
then the next four consecutive numbers are 
$x+1, x+2, x+3, x+4$. 
So, the sum of five numbers is=$5x+10$.\newline
if one number is decreased from the sum new sum is \newline
$(5x+10)\times20\%$\newline
$=(5x+10)\times \frac{20}{100}\newline$
$=(5x+10)\times\frac{1}{5}\newline$
$=x+2$
\newline
We see that $x+2$ was our 3rd consecutive number. \newline We conclude that 3rd number that is \textbf{C} was  removed from the set


\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{2. If the sum of 5 Consecutive Integers is M, What is the largest integer of the set in terms of M?}  \emph{[BB(officer)-2015]} \newline \textbf{Solution:}
\end{itemize}
\begin{center}
    
    Let the first Integer be x. Then the four following consecutive integers are $x+1$, $x+2$, $x+3$, $x+4$ \newline
    sum of the 5 integers is\newline
    $x+x+1+x+2+x+3+x+4=M\newline
    or,5x+10=M\newline or, 5x=M-10\newline or,x=\frac{M-10}{5}$\newline
    We have our largest number $x+4$ that is \newline
    $x+4=\frac{M-10}{5}+4\newline
    or, x+4 =\frac{M-10+20}{5}\newline
    or, x+4=\frac{M+10}{5}
    $\newline
    The largest Number $x+4$ interms of M is $\frac{M+10}{5}$
\end{center}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{3. An old man Distributed all his gold coins to his two sons into two different numbers such that the difference between the squares of the two numbers is 36 times the difference between the two numbers. How many coins did the old man had? }\newline\emph{[SIBL(TO CASH) 2013]}
    \newline\textbf{Solution:\newline}
\end{itemize}

    Let us assume that the old man had $x$  $^*$ coins. first son had "a"  coins and second son had "b" coins.\newline So first son has "a" coins and second son has $b=x-a$ coins and $a+b=x$
    as the question suggests,\newline
    \begin{center}
    $a^{2}-b^{2}=36(a-b)\newline 
    or,    (a+b)(a-b)=36(a-b)\newline
    or, (a+b)=36$\newline

\end{center}\textbf{The old man had total 36 coins.}

 \begin{itemize}
     \item \textbf{4. The difference between two numbers is 5 and the difference between their squares is 65. What is the largest number?} \emph{[BB(Oficer)-2015]}\end{itemize} 
         \textbf{Solution:}
     let the largest number be 'a' and the smallest number be 'b'. that is $a>b$\newline
     Given 
         $a-b=5\dotfill(4.1) \newline \newline a^{2}-b^{2}=65$\dotfill(4.2)
     
 We know that\newline
 \begin{center}
     $a^{2}-b^{2}=(a+b)(a-b)\newline
     or, 65=(a+b)\times5\newline
     or, (a+b)=\frac{65}{5}\newline
     or, (a+b)=13\newline
     (a+b)\dotfill(4.3)$
     \newline  \end{center}
     Adding (4.1) and (4.3) we obtain\newline
     $(a+b)+(a-b)=13+5
     \newline
     (a+b)+(a-b)=18\newline
     or, 2a=18\newline 
     or, a=9\dotfill(4.4)
     \newline $
     from equation (4.1) we have, \newline
     $
     a-b=5\newline
     or, 9-b=5\newline
     or, b=9-5\newline or, b=4
     \newline
     b=4\dotfill(4.5)
     $ \newline
\textbf{     the Largest number A is equal 9 and \newline the Smallest number B is equal 4}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{5. The difference between a two digit number and the number obtained by interchanging the digits is 36. What is the difference between the sum and the difference of the digits of the number if the ratio between the digits of the number is $1:2$?}\emph{[PKB(JEO)-2014]}
    
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Solution:}
Let us assume the unit place digit be x and the Hundreth placed number is y.\newline Then the number is $10y+x$.\newline
After interchanging the digits we have  $10x+y$\newline
Given, \newline
 $(10y+x)-(10x+y)=36\newline
or, 10y-y-10x+x=36\newline
or, 9y-9x=36\newline
or,9(y-x)=36\newline
or, y-x=4\dotfill(5.1)\newline
$
and the ratio between x and y is 1:2 that is \newline
$\frac{x}{y}=\frac{1}{2}\newline
$or,$ 2x=y\dotfill(5.2)\newline$
Inserting the value in equation (5.1) we obtain,\newline
$y-x=4\newline
or, 2x-x=4\newline
or,x=4\newline$
and\newline
$y=2x\newline
or, y =2\times4\newline
or, y=8$

Now the sum of the digits $8+4=12$\newline
The difference of the digits $8-4=4$\newline
\textbf{The Difference between the sum and the difference is 12-4=8}

\begin{itemize}
    \item  \textbf{6. A two digit number becomes five-sixth of itself when its digits are reversed. The two digits differ by one. What is the number? }\emph{[Please Notify Writer if you find reference.]}
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Solution:}
\newline
we emphasise on the fact that "\emph{The two digits differ by one}"\newline
if one digit is x, the other digit is x+1(=y we assume.).        \newline
The required number is 10y+x, as the number decreases when the digits are reversed. We are sure that the hundredth place on the number is y. \newline
Given,        \newline
$(10x+y)=\frac{5}{6} \times(10y+x)    \newline    
or , 6(10x+y) =5(10y+x)\newline
or , 60x+6y=50y+5x\newline
or, 55x=44y\newline
or, 55x=44(x+1)\newline
or,55x-44x=44\newline
or,11x=44\newline
or, x=4$
First digit is 4, then the second digit y=x+1=4+1=5.    \newline
\textbf{So, the number is 10y+x=10$\times$5+4=54}

    \item \textbf{7. A number consists of two digits. The sum of the digit is 9. If 63 is subtracted from the number, its digits interchange. Find the number. }\emph{[notify writer if you find reference]}
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Solution:}\newline
let the digits be a and b and the relation between a \& b be\newline
$a+b=9$\newline
$a=9-b$\dotfill(7.1)\newline
let the number be $10a+b$
Given,\newline
$(10a+b)-63=10b+a$
or, $10a-a-63=10b-b$ \newline 
$or, 9a-9b=63 \newline$\newline
$or, 9(a-b)=63 \newline $\newline
$or, a-b=7 \newline $\newline
$or, 9-b-b=7 \newline $\newline
$or, 9-2b-7=0 \newline $\newline
$or,9-7=2b \newline$\newline
$or,b=1$\newline
Using equation (7.1) we obtain,\newline
$a=9-b \newline
or, a=9-1\newline
or, a=8$\newline
\textbf{The number is 10a+b=10$\times$8+1=81}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{8. If 5 is added to the sum of two digits of a number consisting of two digits, the sum will be three times the digits of  the tenth place. Moreover, If the place of the digits are interchanged, The number thus found will be 9 less than the original number. Find the number\newline }
    \emph{[DB(MTO)-2016, HBFCL(SO)-2015]}

    
\end{itemize}
    \textbf{\newline Solution:\newline}
    let us suppose that the number is 10a+b.\newline
    Given,\newline
    $(a+b)+5=3a\newline
or,    2a=b+5\dotfill (8.1)\newline
again,\newline
(10b+a)+9=10a+b\newline
or, 9b+9=9a\newline
or,b+1=a\newline
or, b+1=\frac{b+5}{2}\newline
or, b+1=\frac{b}{2}+\frac{5}{2}\newline
or,b-\frac{b}{2}=\frac{5}{2}-1\newline
or, \frac{b}{2}=\frac{5-2}{2}\newline
or,b=2\times \frac{3}{2}\newline
or, b=3$\newline
Which gives us 2a=b+5=3+5=8\newline
and a=$\frac{8}{2}=4$\newline
\textbf{The number is 10a+b=10$\times$4+3=43}
